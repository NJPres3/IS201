Tiger Woods and His Impact on the Game of Golf

Introduction

Tiger Woods is a name that is synonymous with the game of golf. A young boy from california grew up to captivate the golf world through his unrelenting work ethic, mental strength, and domination of the game at every level. As a junior, amateur, and, eventually professional, Tiger Woods achieve every accolade and set numerous records that still stand today. His leadership and dominance in the game inspired many others to pick up the game, transforming it from a niche pastime to a global spectacle. He has forever altered the game of golf, and his legacy will continue to influence the sport for generations to come.

Early Life/Junior and Amateur Golf

Tiger grew up in Orange County, California, and was introduced to the game of golf at the age of two by his father Earl, a retired military man. Tiger was a prodigy from an early age. During his junior golf days he won three consecutive U.S. Junior Amatuer Championships in 1991, 1992, 1993. His success continued as a went to play for Stanford University, where he won the 1996 NCAA Individual Championship after having won three consecutive U.S. Amateur Championships in 1994, 1995, 1996. Shortly after, he turned professional, and his story of success continued.

The Greatest Season Ever Played

His most dominant season was in 2000-2001, when he not only won 9 tournaments, but he also complete was become known as the tiger slam. A grand slam in golf is winning all four majors (The Masters, U.S. Open, The Open Championship, and The PGA Championship) in a single year. A career grand slam is considered winning one of each of these tournaments sometime throughout your career. While a handful of legends have completed the career grand slam, none have ever completed the grand slam. Tiger Woods was the closest to do so in 2000-2001 when he won the 2000 U.S. Open, 2000 Open Championship, 2000 PGA Championship, and the 2001 Masters, holding all four majors at the same time: an accomplishment that has become known as the tiger slam. His dominance was not just in the majors, Tiger seemed to win almost every regular PGA tour event that he showed up to. His mental fortitude was much stronger that his opponents and his intense dedication to practice earned him a textbook perfect swing that outmatch all other players in the field. List of His Most Coveted Achievements

Tiger has always said that his favorite victories are his majors, as they are the biggest and most difficult tournaments to win in golf. Next, is what he considers the fifth major, The Players Championship. After that, the World Golf Championships. Here is a list of each of these tournaments and the years he won them:

  1. The Masters
    • Won: 1997, 2001, 2002, 2005, 2019

  2. The U.S. Open
    • Won: 2000, 2002, 2008

  3. The Open Championship
    • Won: 2000, 2005, 2006

  4. PGA Championship
    • Won: 1999, 2000, 2006, 2007

  5. The Players Championship
    • Won: 2001, 2013

  6. The WGC Championship
    • Won: 1999, 2002, 2003, 2005, 2006, 2007, 2013

  7. The WGC Match Play
    • Won: 2003, 2004, 2008

  8. The WGC Invitational
    • Won: 1999, 2000, 2001, 2005, 2006, 2007, 2009, 2013
